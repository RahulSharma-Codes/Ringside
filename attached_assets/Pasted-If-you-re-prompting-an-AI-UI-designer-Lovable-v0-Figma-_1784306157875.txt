If you’re prompting an AI UI designer (Lovable, v0, Figma AI, Claude, Cursor, etc.), the biggest mistake is describing screens instead of describing the product philosophy. Modern fintech apps like Ramp, Mercury, Revolut, Linear, Stripe Dashboard, Raycast, Arc, Notion Calendar, and Perplexity all feel premium because of motion, hierarchy, micro-interactions, and information density—not just colors.

Here’s a much stronger prompt.

⸻

Design Brief — World-Class Inorganic Growth Tracker

Design a premium enterprise web application that feels like a combination of Linear, Mercury, Ramp, Notion, Arc Browser and Apple Human Interface Design.

The application is an Inorganic Growth Operating System used by Corporate Development teams to source, evaluate, track and execute M&A transactions.

This should not look like Salesforce, Monday.com or Airtable.

It should feel like software executives actually enjoy opening every morning.

⸻

Design Principles

Create an interface that is

• Calm
• Premium
• Information dense without clutter
• Beautiful motion
• Extremely fast
• Minimal chrome
• Context aware
• AI-first
• Keyboard-first
• Mobile companion ready

Every interaction should feel intentional.

Use modern glass effects sparingly.

Avoid boring enterprise tables.

Every page should feel alive.

Final Design Goal

If shown without context, users should assume this product was built by a world-class team from Stripe, Linear, Apple, Mercury, Notion, or Ramp.

Prioritize timeless design over flashy effects. Every animation should communicate information, reinforce user actions, or improve orientation—not exist purely for decoration. The interface should remain highly performant, accessible, and scalable even with thousands of companies, hundreds of simultaneous deals, and years of historical data.
